    Arunachal Pradesh

    Deen Dayal Swavalamban Yojana

    To encourage unemployed youth to gain access to low cost capital for entrepreneurship

    • 30% back ended capital investment subsidy for a loan of Rs. 10 Lakhs up to 1 crore
    • Additional 5% interest subsidy annually to women who do not become Non-Performing Asset
    • Entrepreneurs required to be registered under Stand-Up India Scheme
    • Sectors covered - Value addition in Agri-horti and allied sectors; Eco Tourism, Traditional Textile weaving and small scale manufacturing units
    • Entrepreneurs to contribute minimum 10% of project cost
    • Banks to claim for subsidy from NABARD

    Department of Finance, Arunachal Pradesh

    Financial Support

    Arunachal Pradesh

    Deen Dayal Upadhyaya BunkarYojana

    To encourage women weavers to access affordable credit from banks for working capital requirements

    • 7% interest subvention up to 2 Lakh Rs. on short term credit/ cash credit limit/ working capital limit/weavers credit card/ Swarojgar credit card
    • Only individual woman or a group of women weaver having a weaver certificate from the concerned CO/EAC/BDO/SDO/ADC/DC Office is eligible not any company or partnership firm
    • Can be applied through any commercial or regional rural bank
    • NABARD to act as channel partner for reimbursement of interest subvention

    Department of Finance, Arunachal Pradesh

    Financial Support

    Micro Credit Scheme through Haryana Women Development Corporation

    Financial support to SHGs is provided in form of subsidy

    • Loan from RMK (Rashtriya Mahila Kosh) @ 8% p.a. interest to give loan to SHGs and the individual members @ 5% p.a. interest. The balance of 3% interest provided by the Haryana Women Development Corporation

    SARANYA(Self Employment Scheme for the Destitute Women)

    Empowers most backward and segregated women by providing interest free loans

    • Focuses on uplifting the most backward and segregated women in the State, namely widows, divorced, deserted, spinsters above the age of 30 and unwedded mothers of Scheduled Tribe

    Kerala

    KESRU(Kerala Self Employment Scheme for the Registered Unemployed

    Financial aid for starting self-employment ventures to individuals

    • Bank loan up to Rs. 1,00,000 for starting self-employment ventures- 20% reimbursed as government subsidy. Beneficiary contribution not mandatory
    • Unemployed persons between 21-50 registered in employment exchanges with annual family income not exceeding Rs.1,00,000/- eligible
    • Preference to people with professional/technical qualification, woman having graduation

    MPSC/JC (Multi-Purpose Service Centres/Job clubs)

    Maharashtra

    Maharashtra

    Industrial Policy for Women Entrepreneurship

    Comprehensive support for encouraging women entrepreneurship

    • First-generation women entrepreneurs setting up MSMEs entitled to capital subsidy of up to Rs 1 crore, depending on location of the unit
    • Other incentives- extended lower power tariffs (₹1-2/unit), higher interest subsides (~5%), and grants for branding their products (₹1crore over 5 years)
    • Building spaces exclusively for women-run enterprises in malls, commercial places; incentive scheme for promoting women-run incubation centres; up to 90 per cent grants for industrial clusters catering exclusively to women-run enterprises
    • Women self-help groups can avail these incentives provided they register as a company under the Companies Act
